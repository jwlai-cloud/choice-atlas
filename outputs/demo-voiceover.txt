We all hit forks in the road. Take the new job, or stay. Move to another city, or keep the life you've built. And when we go looking for help, we usually get one of two things — someone confidently telling us what to do, as if they knew our life. Or a flat list of pros and cons that strips all the feeling out of it. Neither one really helps.

What actually helps is seeing the decision clearly. What do you already know for sure? What are you only assuming? And what is genuinely unknown — the part nobody can promise you? That's what Choice Atlas shows you — an app we designed and built with Codex as our coding agent, and powered live by GPT-5.6.

It starts with you. You type in the two real routes you're weighing, in your own words — like taking a job offer in another city, or staying near the family and friends you love. Any true fork in your life fits into these two fields.

Then you tell it what actually matters to you here — things like creative depth, belonging, or financial runway — up to three. And you set a time horizon: three months, one year, or three years.

Now the important part. Choice Atlas hands your two routes to GPT-5.6, and the model works through them live — right now, for your exact situation. This is not a canned template. Every map is reasoned on the spot, from the routes, the priorities, and the horizon you gave it.

And the first thing you see isn't a wall of text — it's a picture. Two currents are your two routes. Where they cross is the real tension between them. The solid points are known ground. And the fog? That's honest — it marks what simply can't be known yet. The model maps your uncertainty; it never pretends to predict your future.

From there it walks you through it, one piece at a time. First, a single trade-off. A trade-off isn't you failing to decide — it's a real cost pulling both ways: what one route gives you is often what the other route asks you to give up.

Then a question worth investigating — something concrete that could actually change the picture, like shadowing an ordinary workday before you commit. And a route people forget: "Not yet." A small, reversible test, to gather real evidence before a big, hard-to-undo choice.

Finally, the whole landscape opens up, and you explore it at your own pace. Hover any point to read its note. Solid marks are grounded in what you told it. Softer marks are assumptions worth checking. Fog is what stays unknown. Your priorities shift the emphasis — so this map is the shape of your decision, not a generic one.

Under the surface, GPT-5.6 returns a structured map that's checked and validated before it ever reaches the screen. And Codex was our coding agent for the whole build — it wrote the test-driven safety contract, the visual system, the server integration, and the thirty-eight automated tests that keep it honest. GPT-5.6 maps the terrain, live; it never tells you which way to go.

Choice Atlas can't decide your life for you. But it can help you see the true shape of the choice — and make it with more care.
